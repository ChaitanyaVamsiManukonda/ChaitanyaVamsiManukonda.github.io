Modeling and Analysis of Non-Pneumatic Tyres with Different Design Structures Using FEM Method

January 2021 - July 2021

Abstract

The project is centered around the analysis of non-pneumatic tires, innovatively designed to circumvent the drawbacks associated with traditional pneumatic tires. This groundbreaking endeavor replaces the conventional reliance on air pressure with a robust, airless system, aiming to eliminate issues such as punctures and the constant need for pressure maintenance. Our focus is mainly on analyzing the efficiency of different structures of the designs by simulation and finding out the best design.

Initial Concept and Design

The project commenced with the creation of various structural models using CATIA software, focusing particularly on geometric configurations such as hexagonal shapes. These shapes were chosen based on their potential to evenly distribute stress and reduce the strain on the tire under operational conditions. Each model was meticulously crafted to explore different aspects of flexibility and load-bearing efficiency.

Material Selection

The selection of materials was critical to the success of the tire design. The chosen materials needed to be robust yet flexible enough to handle varying terrain without failure. A combination of advanced polymeric materials known for their high tensile strength and durability was selected. These materials were also evaluated for their environmental resistance, such as their ability to withstand temperature variations and exposure to chemicals, ensuring the tire's longevity and performance stability.

Simulation and Analysis

Following the design phase, the project moved into extensive simulation using Ansys software. This phase was crucial as it involved detailed analysis of how the tires would perform under different stress conditions, including load distribution, flexibility under compression, and their ability to return to original shapes after deformation. The simulations provided valuable insights into the potential failure points and allowed for the adjustment of the tire's design to optimize its performance. Stress analysis and deformation studies were particularly emphasized to ensure that the tires could withstand real-world operational stresses without failure.

Technical Documentation and Insights

The final phase of the project involved compiling detailed technical documentation that included all design processes, material specifications, and analysis results. This documentation is vital for guiding future development and providing a foundation for scaling the tire design for production. The project has laid the groundwork for a new generation of tires that promises increased safety and efficiency. By focusing on innovative design and thorough analysis, the project highlights a substantial advancement in tire technology, paving the way for future enhancements that could redefine vehicular mobility across various terrains and conditions.

Conclusion

The project on non-pneumatic tires demonstrated significant advancements by replacing conventional pneumatic designs with innovative, airless structures, effectively addressing issues like punctures and pressure maintenance. Using CATIA and Ansys, various geometric configurations were evaluated for their stress distribution and load-bearing efficiency, with advanced polymeric materials ensuring durability and environmental resistance. This research paves the way for safer, more efficient tires, potentially transforming vehicular mobility across diverse terrains.
